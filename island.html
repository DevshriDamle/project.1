Pristine Island - Lakshadweep

Location:

Lakshadweep is a group of islands located in the Laccadive Sea, off the southwestern coast of India. It is known for its stunning coral reefs, clear blue waters, and pristine beaches.

Nearby Attractions:

  • Agatti Island: Known for its beautiful lagoons and water sports activities.
  • Bangaram Island: A secluded island offering serene beaches and excellent diving spots.
  • Kavaratti Island: The administrative capital, known for its marine aquarium and scenic beauty.
  • Minicoy Island: Famous for its lighthouse, traditional lava dance, and tuna canning factory.
  • Kalpeni Island: Known for its scenic beauty, water sports, and rich marine life.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Agatti Airport is the main airport connecting Lakshadweep to the mainland, primarily via Kochi.
  • Sea Travel: Passenger ships operate from Kochi to various islands in Lakshadweep, providing an alternative mode of travel.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget guesthouses range from ₹2,000 to ₹4,000 per night. Mid-range resorts cost between ₹5,000 to ₹10,000 per night, while luxury resorts can go upwards of ₹15,000 per night.
  • Food: Casual dining can cost around ₹500 to ₹1,500 per meal. Resorts may offer meal packages ranging from ₹2,000 to ₹5,000 per day.
  • Transportation: Local transport includes ferries and boats. Inter-island travel is mainly by speedboat or helicopter (for certain islands).
  • Activities: Entry fees and charges for activities such as snorkeling, diving, and water sports. Budget for around ₹2,000 to ₹5,000 per day for activities.

Exotic Island - Andaman

Location:

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of islands located in the Bay of Bengal. Known for their tropical rainforests, white sandy beaches, and vibrant coral reefs, they are a popular tourist destination.

Nearby Attractions:

  • Radhanagar Beach: Often listed as one of the best beaches in Asia, located on Havelock Island.
  • Cellular Jail: A historic colonial prison in Port Blair, now a national memorial.
  • Ross Island: Former administrative headquarters of the British, now a historical site with ruins and wildlife.
  • Neil Island: Known for its coral reefs, sandy beaches, and lush greenery.
  • Baratang Island: Famous for its limestone caves and mud volcanoes.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Veer Savarkar International Airport in Port Blair is the main airport connecting the Andaman Islands to mainland India.
  • Sea Travel: Regular passenger ships operate from Chennai, Kolkata, and Visakhapatnam to Port Blair.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget guesthouses range from ₹1,500 to ₹3,000 per night. Mid-range resorts cost between ₹4,000 to ₹8,000 per night, while luxury resorts can go upwards of ₹10,000 per night.
  • Food: Casual dining can cost around ₹300 to ₹1,000 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹1,200 to ₹2,500 for a meal for two, while fine dining can exceed ₹3,000.
  • Transportation: Local transport includes auto-rickshaws, taxis, and ferries for inter-island travel. Helicopter services are also available for certain routes.
  • Activities: Entry fees and charges for activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, and island tours. Budget for around ₹1,500 to ₹4,000 per day for activities.
