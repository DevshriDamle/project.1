Charming Village - Shimla

Location:

Shimla, the capital city of Himachal Pradesh, is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Himalayas. It is known for its colonial architecture, lush greenery, and pleasant climate.

Nearby Attractions:

  • The Ridge: A spacious open space in the heart of Shimla, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Mall Road: A bustling shopping street lined with shops, cafes, and restaurants.
  • Jakhoo Temple: An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, located atop Jakhoo Hill.
  • Christ Church: The second oldest church in North India, known for its stunning stained-glass windows.
  • Kufri: A small hill station near Shimla, famous for its skiing and hiking trails.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Shimla Airport, located around 22 km from the city center.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Shimla Railway Station, well connected to major cities via the Kalka-Shimla toy train.
  • Bus Stations: Regular bus services are available from Delhi, Chandigarh, and other nearby cities to Shimla.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget guesthouses range from ₹1,500 to ₹3,000 per night. Mid-range hotels cost between ₹4,000 to ₹7,000 per night, while luxury hotels can go upwards of ₹10,000 per night.
  • Food: Casual dining can cost around ₹300 to ₹800 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹1,000 to ₹2,000 for a meal for two, while fine dining can exceed ₹3,000.
  • Transportation: Local transport includes taxis, auto-rickshaws, and buses. Toy train rides are also popular among tourists.
  • Activities: Entry fees to attractions and charges for activities such as trekking, skiing, and sightseeing. Budget for around ₹1,000 to ₹3,000 per day for activities.

Serene Village - Diskit

Location:

Diskit is a tranquil village located in the Nubra Valley of Ladakh, India. It is famous for its scenic landscapes, ancient monasteries, and cultural richness.

Nearby Attractions:

  • Diskit Monastery: The oldest and largest Buddhist monastery in Nubra Valley, known for its impressive statue of Maitreya Buddha.
  • Hunder Sand Dunes: A unique cold desert with rolling sand dunes and opportunities for camel rides.
  • Sumur Village: A nearby village known for its Samstanling Monastery and serene environment.
  • Panamik: Famous for its hot springs and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Khardung La: One of the highest motorable passes in the world, offering breathtaking views and an adventurous drive.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport in Leh, approximately 120 km from Diskit.
  • Road Travel: The village is accessible by road from Leh via the Khardung La pass. Taxis and private vehicles are commonly used.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget guesthouses range from ₹1,000 to ₹2,500 per night. Mid-range hotels cost between ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 per night, while luxury accommodations can go upwards of ₹7,000 per night.
  • Food: Casual dining can cost around ₹200 to ₹600 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹800 to ₹1,500 for a meal for two, while fine dining options are limited.
  • Transportation: Local transport includes taxis and shared vehicles. Private vehicle rentals are also available in Leh.
  • Activities: Entry fees to attractions and charges for activities such as camel rides, monastery visits, and sightseeing. Budget for around ₹500 to ₹2,000 per day for activities.
