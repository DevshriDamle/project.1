Scenic Hill Station - Nainital

Location:

Nainital is a picturesque hill station located in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, India. It is famous for its scenic beauty, serene lakes, and lush greenery.

Nearby Attractions:

  • Naini Lake: A beautiful, crescent-shaped lake surrounded by hills, perfect for boating.
  • Naina Devi Temple: A revered Hindu temple located on the northern shore of Naini Lake.
  • Tiffin Top: A popular viewpoint offering panoramic views of Nainital and the surrounding mountains.
  • Snow View Point: Accessible by cable car, this viewpoint offers breathtaking views of snow-capped Himalayan peaks.
  • Eco Cave Gardens: A series of interconnected caves and hanging gardens that are fun to explore.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Pantnagar Airport, approximately 70 km from Nainital.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Kathgodam Railway Station, located around 34 km from Nainital.
  • Bus Stations: Nainital is well-connected by road. Regular bus services are available from major cities like Delhi, Dehradun, and Kathgodam.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget hotels and guesthouses range from ₹1,000 to ₹2,500 per night. Mid-range hotels cost between ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 per night, while luxury resorts can go upwards of ₹7,000 per night.
  • Food: Street food and casual dining can cost around ₹300 to ₹700 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹700 to ₹1,500 for a meal for two, while fine dining can exceed ₹2,000.
  • Transportation: Local taxis and shared jeeps are widely available. Renting a car can range from ₹2,000 to ₹4,000 per day.
  • Activities: Boating, trekking, and sightseeing tours range from ₹500 to ₹2,000 per activity.

Scenic Hill Station - Dehradun

Location:

Dehradun is the capital city of Uttarakhand, India, and is nestled in the Doon Valley between the Himalayan foothills and the Shivalik range. It is known for its pleasant climate and picturesque landscape.

Nearby Attractions:

  • Robber's Cave: A natural cave formation with a river running through it, ideal for picnics and short treks.
  • Sahastradhara: A natural sulphur spring and popular tourist spot known for its therapeutic properties.
  • Forest Research Institute: A historic institute with grand colonial architecture and lush grounds, offering a glimpse into India's forestry history.
  • Tapkeshwar Temple: A cave temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on the banks of a seasonal river.
  • Malsi Deer Park: A small zoological park located at the base of the Shivalik range, home to deer, peacocks, and other wildlife.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant Airport, approximately 30 km from Dehradun.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Dehradun Railway Station, located in the heart of the city.
  • Bus Stations: Dehradun is well-connected by road. Regular bus services are available from major cities like Delhi, Haridwar, and Rishikesh.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget hotels and guesthouses range from ₹1,000 to ₹2,500 per night. Mid-range hotels cost between ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 per night, while luxury resorts can go upwards of ₹7,000 per night.
  • Food: Street food and casual dining can cost around ₹300 to ₹700 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹700 to ₹1,500 for a meal for two, while fine dining can exceed ₹2,000.
  • Transportation: Local taxis and auto-rickshaws are widely available. Renting a car can range from ₹2,000 to ₹4,000 per day.
  • Activities: Short treks, sightseeing tours, and nature walks range from ₹500 to ₹2,000 per activity.
