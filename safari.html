Wildlife Safari - Jim Corbett National Park

Location:

Jim Corbett National Park is located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand, India. It is the oldest national park in India, established in 1936, and is known for its rich biodiversity and the presence of Bengal tigers.

Nearby Attractions:

  • Dhikala Zone: The most famous safari zone in the park, known for its stunning landscape and abundant wildlife.
  • Garjia Temple: A picturesque temple located on a large rock in the middle of the Kosi River.
  • Corbett Museum: A museum dedicated to the life and work of Jim Corbett, the renowned hunter and conservationist.
  • Bijrani Zone: Another popular safari zone, known for its open grasslands and high chances of spotting tigers.
  • Kalagarh Dam: A scenic dam offering beautiful views and bird-watching opportunities.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Pantnagar Airport, approximately 80 km from Jim Corbett National Park.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Ramnagar Railway Station, located around 12 km from the park.
  • Bus Stations: Regular bus services are available from Delhi, Nainital, and other nearby cities to Ramnagar.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget lodges and guesthouses range from ₹1,500 to ₹3,000 per night. Mid-range resorts cost between ₹4,000 to ₹6,000 per night, while luxury wildlife resorts can go upwards of ₹8,000 per night.
  • Food: Casual dining at lodges and nearby restaurants can cost around ₹300 to ₹800 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹1,000 to ₹2,500 for a meal for two, while fine dining can exceed ₹3,000.
  • Transportation: Local taxis and jeeps for safari tours are widely available. Safari costs vary but typically range from ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 per jeep safari (for up to 6 people).
  • Activities: Entry fees to the park and safari tours. Budget for around ₹1,500 to ₹3,500 per safari tour.

Wildlife Safari - Pench National Park

Location:

Pench National Park is located in the Seoni and Chhindwara districts of Madhya Pradesh, India. It is known for its diverse wildlife and served as the inspiration for Rudyard Kipling's famous novel "The Jungle Book".

Nearby Attractions:

  • Turiya Gate: The main entry gate to Pench National Park, known for its high density of wildlife.
  • Karmajhiri Gate: Another entry point offering excellent safari experiences and wildlife sightings.
  • Pachmarhi: A beautiful hill station nearby, known for its waterfalls, caves, and serene environment.
  • Pench River: The lifeline of the park, providing picturesque views and excellent bird-watching opportunities.
  • Rookhad Wildlife Sanctuary: A nearby sanctuary offering additional wildlife viewing opportunities and nature trails.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport, Nagpur, approximately 90 km from Pench National Park.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Seoni Railway Station, located around 30 km from the park.
  • Bus Stations: Regular bus services are available from Nagpur, Jabalpur, and other nearby cities to Pench.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget lodges and guesthouses range from ₹1,500 to ₹3,000 per night. Mid-range resorts cost between ₹4,000 to ₹6,000 per night, while luxury wildlife resorts can go upwards of ₹8,000 per night.
  • Food: Casual dining at lodges and nearby restaurants can cost around ₹300 to ₹800 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹1,000 to ₹2,500 for a meal for two, while fine dining can exceed ₹3,000.
