Vibrant City - Mumbai

Location:

Mumbai, also known as Bombay, is the capital city of the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is the financial, commercial, and entertainment hub of India, located on the west coast along the Arabian Sea.

Nearby Attractions:

  • Gateway of India: A historic monument built during the 20th century in Mumbai, overlooking the Arabian Sea.
  • Marine Drive: A famous promenade along the coast, offering stunning sunset views and a beautiful nighttime cityscape.
  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus: A UNESCO World Heritage site and historic railway station known for its Victorian Gothic architecture.
  • Elephanta Caves: Ancient rock-cut caves located on Elephanta Island, featuring sculptures and artwork dedicated to Hindu gods.
  • Juhu Beach: One of Mumbai's most popular beaches, known for its lively atmosphere and street food.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport, located approximately 20 km from the city center.
  • Nearest Railway Stations:
    • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT), located in the heart of the city.
    • Mumbai Central Railway Station, another major station serving long-distance and suburban trains.
  • Bus Stations: Mumbai has a vast network of buses operated by BEST (Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport). Major bus terminals include Mumbai Central and Dadar.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget hotels and guesthouses range from ₹1,500 to ₹3,000 per night. Mid-range hotels cost between ₹4,000 to ₹7,000 per night, while luxury hotels can go upwards of ₹10,000 per night.
  • Food: Street food and casual dining can cost around ₹300 to ₹800 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹1,000 to ₹2,500 for a meal for two, while fine dining can exceed ₹3,500.
  • Transportation: Local taxis, auto-rickshaws, and the suburban railway network are widely available. Ride-sharing services and car rentals are also popular.
  • Activities: Entry fees to attractions and activities vary. Budget for around ₹1,000 to ₹3,000 per day for sightseeing and activities.

Historic City - Chennai

Location:

Chennai, formerly known as Madras, is the capital city of the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It is located on the southeastern coast of India along the Bay of Bengal and is known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant arts scene.

Nearby Attractions:

  • Marina Beach: One of the longest urban beaches in the world, stretching along the Bay of Bengal.
  • Fort St. George: A historic fort built by the British East India Company, now housing a museum and government offices.
  • Kapaleeshwarar Temple: A stunning Dravidian-style temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its intricate architecture.
  • Government Museum: One of the oldest museums in India, featuring a vast collection of artifacts and art.
  • San Thome Basilica: A basilica built over the tomb of St. Thomas the Apostle, showcasing beautiful neo-Gothic architecture.

Mode of Travel:

  • Nearest Airport: Chennai International Airport, located approximately 20 km from the city center.
  • Nearest Railway Stations:
    • Chennai Central Railway Station, the main railway hub in the city.
    • Chennai Egmore Railway Station, serving many intercity and suburban trains.
  • Bus Stations: Chennai has an extensive bus network operated by the Metropolitan Transport Corporation (MTC). Major bus terminals include CMBT (Chennai Mofussil Bus Terminus) and Broadway.

Overall Travelling Expenses:

  • Accommodation: Budget hotels and guesthouses range from ₹1,200 to ₹2,500 per night. Mid-range hotels cost between ₹3,000 to ₹6,000 per night, while luxury hotels can go upwards of ₹8,000 per night.
  • Food: Street food and casual dining can cost around ₹200 to ₹600 per meal. Mid-range restaurants might charge between ₹800 to ₹2,000 for a meal for two, while fine dining can exceed ₹3,000.
  • Transportation: Local taxis, auto-rickshaws, and the suburban railway network are widely available. Ride-sharing services and car rentals are also popular.
  • Activities: Entry fees to attractions and activities vary. Budget for around ₹800 to ₹2,500 per day for sightseeing and activities.
